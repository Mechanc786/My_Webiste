Dubai Real Estate Investment FAQ - Common Questions

Yes, foreigners can buy freehold properties in Dubai in designated areas including Downtown Dubai, Dubai Marina, Palm Jumeirah, and many other locations. Foreign ownership is 100% allowed for apartments, villas, and commercial properties in freehold areas.
The minimum property investment for Dubai Golden Visa is AED 2 million (approximately $545,000 USD). This investment qualifies you for a 10-year renewable UAE Golden Visa with residency benefits for you and your family.
Dubai properties typically offer rental yields of 6-12% annually, depending on location and property type. Dubai Marina and Downtown Dubai apartments often achieve 8-10% yields, while some emerging areas can offer even higher returns.
Dubai has no property tax, no capital gains tax, and no income tax on rental income. You only pay a one-time 4% Dubai Land Department fee during property registration and annual service charges for property maintenance.
The Dubai property buying process typically takes 2-4 weeks from offer acceptance to completion. This includes property viewing, offer negotiation, mortgage approval (if applicable), and final registration with Dubai Land Department.
Top Dubai investment areas include Downtown Dubai (Burj Khalifa area), Dubai Marina, Palm Jumeirah, Business Bay, Dubai Hills Estate, and upcoming areas like Dubai South and Mohammed Bin Rashid City. Each offers different investment potential and rental yields.
